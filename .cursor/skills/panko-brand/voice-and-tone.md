# Panko Studio — Voice and Tone

This is the long form. For the quick-reference cheat sheet, see
[SKILL.md](SKILL.md).

## The voice in one sentence

Panko sounds like a **senior PM/designer who's been through it** — calm,
direct, slightly understated. We do not sound like a hype agency.

## Three signature moves

### 1. Confident statement, accent period

Short, declarative, ends in a period (the period is rendered in
`#0081A7`). Use this for headlines, section openers, and email subjects.

> Design for startups and scale-ups.
> Let's collaborate.
> Transparent investment.
> Built for conversion.
> Shipped, not staged.

### 2. "[Capability] for [audience]"

The lead-line pattern. Pair a strong product noun with a target audience.

> Product management and design for ambitious AI x B2B teams.
> Design systems for teams shipping monthly.
> Wireframe-to-release support for AI founders.

### 3. Plain-spoken value clarification

When you need to say what we actually do. Drop the fluff.

> Senior product talent that drives design projects from wireframe to full release alongside your engineers.
> We don't outsource the thinking. The senior on the call is the senior doing the work.
> Pricing is per project or monthly retainer. No setup fees.

## The Panko period (signature mark)

Every closing-statement headline ends with a period rendered in
`#0081A7`. It's the brand's most repeated visual gesture.

In HTML/CSS:

```html
<h2>Let's collaborate<span style="color:#0081A7;">.</span></h2>
```

In design tools (Figma, slides): select the period glyph specifically
and color it `#0081A7`. The rest of the headline stays brand-ink.

Where to use it:

- Page section headlines
- Email body headlines
- Slide titles (16:9 and one-pagers)
- Social post hook lines (visually faked with a colored em-shape if the
  platform doesn't support color — or just skip; plain text on social is
  fine)

Where **not** to use it:

- Subject lines (read as plain text in inboxes)
- Body paragraphs (only the closing period of a section headline)
- Mid-sentence punctuation

## Do / don't vocabulary

### Use

- "Senior product talent" (not "experts" / "rockstars" / "gurus")
- "Ship", "build", "deliver", "release"
- "Wireframe to release"
- "Alongside your engineers"
- "Calm velocity" (sparingly — only when it earns its place)
- "Discovery call" (for the booking CTA)
- "Project" / "retainer" / "subscription"
- "Inquiry" (in our internal language for inbound leads)

### Avoid

- **Superlatives**: "amazing", "world-class", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "next-gen"
- **Buzzwords**: "leverage", "synergize", "harness", "unlock", "supercharge"
- **Hype openings**: "🚀 Excited to announce", "Thrilled to share", "I'm so pumped"
- **Engagement-bait**: "What do you think?", "Hot take:", "Unpopular opinion:"
- **AI vapor**: "AI-powered" / "AI-first" used as decoration (we work *with* AI teams; we don't sell AI as a feature)
- **Marketing CTAs**: "Don't miss out", "Limited spots", "Act now"

### Punctuation rules

- **Periods**: yes, including on short headlines (and color them accent)
- **Exclamation marks**: no, ever. They read as a hype tone we don't want
- **Em-dashes**: no. We use periods for emphasis. If you need a pause, end the sentence and start a new one
- **Ellipses**: rare. Only in genuinely trailed-off speech, never as a "more to come..." gesture
- **Semicolons**: fine in body paragraphs, never in headlines
- **Smart quotes**: yes, where the rendering tool supports them

## Sentence-level patterns

### Open with the noun, not the feeling

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| We're so excited to share our work on AcmeCo's checkout. | AcmeCo's checkout moves from 4 screens to 1. |
| It was a pleasure partnering with Beta Inc. | Beta Inc shipped a redesigned dashboard in 5 weeks. |
| Thrilled to launch our new pricing page. | New pricing page is live. |

### Cut warm-up paragraphs

Most Panko copy has at least one paragraph that's pure warm-up — saying
we're "going to talk about" something instead of saying it. Strike the
warm-up; lead with the substance.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| In today's competitive landscape, design has become more important than ever. We've been thinking a lot about what makes great product design… | The cheapest place to fix a bad product decision is the wireframe. |

### One idea per sentence

If a sentence has two `and`s in it, break it into two sentences. If you
find yourself reaching for a semicolon, the sentence is probably trying
to do two jobs.

### Specifics over abstractions

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| We help teams move fast. | The first prototype lands in week one. |
| We've worked with great companies. | Twelve teams, mostly Series A through C. |
| Years of experience. | Seven years across product and design. |

## Before / after rewrites

### Email subject line (visitor confirmation)

**Bad**
> 🎉 Thank you SO much for reaching out!!! We can't wait to hear more!

**Good**
> Thanks for reaching out, [Name].

### LinkedIn post (work announcement)

**Bad**
> 🚀 Beyond thrilled to share that the Panko Studio team has been working
> on a HUGE redesign for one of our amazing clients. We can't wait to
> share more! Stay tuned 👀
>
> #design #startups #productdesign

**Good**
> We just wrapped a six-week design partnership with a Series A AI
> infrastructure team.
>
> The brief was simple: make the platform's first 60 seconds feel
> earned. We rewrote onboarding, redesigned three core dashboards, and
> shipped a small design system the engineers can keep extending.
>
> The interesting part was how much of the work was deletion, not
> addition.

### Hero / landing page subhead

**Bad**
> We're a world-class team of designers and product strategists helping
> ambitious companies leverage AI to unlock breakthrough growth.

**Good**
> Product management and design for ambitious AI x B2B teams.

### Pricing description

**Bad**
> Our flagship offering, the Essential package, is perfect for startups
> looking to take their product to the next level with our award-winning
> design expertise.

**Good**
> Design your core product from scratch in 6–8 weeks.

### Internal Slack-style update (yes, even these have a voice)

**Bad**
> Hey team!! Quick update — we got a great new lead today!! Looks like
> they might be interested in chatting!

**Good**
> New inquiry: Jane Doe (AcmeCo). Wants to chat about web app design.
> I'll reply by EOD.

## Reviewing someone else's draft

When asked to review a piece of Panko copy:

1. Mark every exclamation mark, em-dash, and emoji with a strike.
2. Search for the banned vocabulary list above. Suggest replacements.
3. Check every headline ends with the accent period.
4. Check there's no warm-up paragraph before the substance.
5. Check at least one specific number, name, or concrete detail per
   ~150 words.
6. Check sign-off matches: "Talk soon, / The Panko Studio team" for
   one-to-one pieces.

Return feedback in three buckets:

- **Cut** — lines/words to delete outright
- **Tighten** — lines to keep but rewrite for the voice
- **Add** — specifics that would replace generic phrasing
